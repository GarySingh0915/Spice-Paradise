Jalebi

Ingredients

  • Refined flour 1½ cups
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • Large pinch of saffron
  • Pinch of green cardamom powder
  • Ghee for deep frying
  • Chopped pistachios to sprinkle

Steps

  1. Take 1½ cups refined flour in a bowl, add 1½ cups water and beat with hand for 15-20 minutes to make a light smooth batter. Cover the bowl and keep in a warm place to ferment for 12 hours.
  2. Cook sugar with 2 cups water in a deep non-stick pan on high heat, stirring continuously. Add milk, mix well and bring it to boil. Discard the scum that will rise to the top.
  3. Add saffron and green cardamom powder and mix well. Cook till the sugar syrup reaches one string consistency.
  4. Add the remaining flour to the fermented batter and mix well with your hand.
  5. Heat sufficient ghee in a kadai on medium heat. Pour the fermented jalebi batter in jalebi cloth and pipe the spirals directly into moderately hot ghee and deep-fry till crisp.
  6. Drain and soak in sugar syrup for 5-10 minutes. Remove from the sugar syrup and place them on a serving platter. Garnish with pistachios.

Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • 4 cups milk powder
  • 1 cup refined flour
  • Pinch of soda bicarbonate
  • 2 teaspoon oil + for deep frying
  • Yogurt as required
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon green cardamom powder

Steps

  1. Cook the sugar with four cups of water till the sugar dissolves. Add green cardamom power and mix. Keep the syrup warm.
  2. Mix the milk powder, refined flour and soda bicarbonate well together. Add two teaspoons oil and enough yogurt and knead lightly into a soft dough. Take care not to knead the dough too much.
  3. Divide the dough into marble sized balls.
  4. Heat sufficient oil in a kadai and deep fry the balls over medium heat, stirring occasionally, till golden brown. Drain on absorbent paper.
  5. Add the gulab jamuns to the warm syrup and let it soak for about fifteen minutes to half an hour before serving.

Besan Ladoo

Ingredients

  • 4 cups coarse gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup ghee
  • 1 teaspoon green cardamom powder
  • 1 3/4 cups powdered sugar
  • Cashew nut halves for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat ghee in a non-stick wok, add gram flour and mix well. Roast on medium heat for 30-35 minutes or till fragrant and golden brown. Take the pan off the heat, transfer the mixture into a large steel parat and allow to cool down.
  2. Add green cardamom powder and powdered sugar when the mixture is still a little warm and mix till well combined.
  3. For making laddoos, take a portion of the mixture and shape it into a ball. Make a slight dent on top and place a cashewnut half in it and press gently.
  4. Arrange them in a serving platter and serve or store in an airtight container.

Kesar Pista Kulfi

Ingredients

  • Large pinch of saffron
  • 2 tablespoons coarsely crushed pistachios
  • 1 litre full fat milk
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 1 tablespoon chopped cashewnuts
  • Almonds
  • 1 tablespoon cashewnut paste
  • 2 tablespoons condensed milk

Steps

  1. Crush green cardamoms coarsely in a mortar with a pestle. Set aside.
  2. Heat milk in a deep non-stick pan, add crushed cardamoms, mix and boil, stirring frequently, till it reduces to half its quantity.
  3. Discard cardamoms, add pistachios, saffron, cashewnuts, almonds, cashewnut paste and condensed milk, mix well and cook for 5-6 minutes.
  4. Pour the prepared mixture into a jug and further into individual kulfi moulds. Cover the moulds and keep them in the freezer till semi set.
  5. Insert ice cream sticks in the middle of each kulfi and freeze till fully set.
  6. Demould and serve immediately.

Coconut Barfi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups scraped fresh coconut
  • Ghee for greasing
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Pinch of green cardamom powder
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped pistachios
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • Silver varq for garnish
  • Pistachio powder to sprinkle

Steps

  1. Grease a barfi tray with some ghee.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan. Add coconut, sugar, cream and cardamom powder, mix and sauté till the mixture turns golden brown.
  3. Add chopped pistachios and mix well. Switch off heat, add powdered sugar and mix well.
  4. Spread the mixture in the greased tray, garnish with silver varq, sprinkle some pistachio powder on top and set aside to cool.
  5. Cut into squares and serve.

Gajar ka Halwa

Ingredients

  • 5-6 large carrots peeled and grated
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 3/4 cup full fat milk
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • Cashewnuts chopped + for garnish
  • Almonds sliced + for garnish
  • 1/2 teaspoon green cardamom powder

Steps

  1. Heat ghee in a deep non-stick pan. Add carrots, mix and saute for 15-20 minutes or till most of the moisture dries up.
  2. Add milk, a little at a time and mix well. Cover and cook on medium heat for 10 minutes or till the carrots get cooked but still retain a slight crunch.
  3. Add sugar, mix well and cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, for 5-7 minutes or till the moisture dries up.
  4. Add cashew nuts, almonds and cardamom powder, mix well and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Transfer the gajar halwa into a serving bowl, garnish with cashew nuts and almonds and serve hot.

Rasgulla

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • 2-3 pods cardamom

Steps

  1. Heat milk in a pan. Remove from heat once it comes to a boil.
  2. Add lemon juice. The milk will curdle and the whey will separate. Strain the milk in a muslin cloth and collect the cheese. Run the cheese under cold water to remove the sourness from the lemon.
  3. Hang the muslin cloth for an hour to remove excess water from the cheese. Take out the cheese on the work surface and knead for 4-5 minute till it becomes smooth.
  4. Make 10-12 small balls from the cheese. Heat water, sugar and cardamon in a pan. When the water comes to a boil, add the cheese balls in the water.
  5. Cook covered for 40-45 minutes on low heat. Remove from heat and cool. Serve chilled.

Rasmalai

Ingredients

  • 10 Rasgullas (store bought or make your own by referring above)
  • ½ liter milk
  • ½ can sweetened condensed milk
  • Pinch of saffron
  • ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Few drops yellow food color
  • ¼ cup finely chopped cashews or any nuts

Steps

  1. Take rasgulla and squeeze the liquid from it. Set aside.
  2. Take milk, condensed milk in a sauce pan and bring it to a boil. Boil for 10 mins.
  3. Add in rasgullas, kesar and mix well.
  4. Simmer for 5 mins. Add in yellow food colour and cardamom powder and mix well.
  5. Chill till it is nice and cold. Serve.

Kheer

Ingredients

  • 5 cups full cream milk
  • 1/4 cup washed rice
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 10-12 raisins
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 10-12 shredded, blanched almonds

Steps

  1. Boil the rice and milk in a deep pan.
  2. Simmer over low flame, stirring occasionally till the rice is cooked and the milk becomes thick.
  3. When done add sugar, raisins and cardamoms. Stir till sugar gets dissolved properly.
  4. Transfer into a serving dish and garnish with almonds. Serve hot or chilled.
